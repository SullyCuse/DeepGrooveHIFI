A phono preamplifier is one component where spending a little more than the bare minimum pays real dividends. The cheapest phono stages — those built into entry-level turntables or budget receivers — often have elevated noise, imprecise RIAA equalization, and no adjustability. Moving up to a dedicated external unit at $100–$300 transforms what you can hear from even a modestly priced turntable.

## Buying Tips for This Category

**Check your cartridge output first.** Low-output MC cartridges (under 0.5 mV) require specific high-gain phono stages. Most units in this price range handle high-output MC and all MM cartridges easily, but confirm low-output MC support if that's your cartridge.

**The subsonic filter matters more than you might expect.** If your records are even slightly warped — and many are — a good subsonic filter prevents inaudible low-frequency oscillations from eating up amplifier headroom. Every unit on this list includes one.

**Internal DIP switches vs. external switches.** Some units require you to open the chassis and adjust DIP switches for loading. Others have external controls. If you're experimenting with MC loading, external controls are far more convenient.

**Power supply quality varies.** A wall-wart power supply is common at this price. A linear regulated supply (or quality switching supply) makes a real difference to noise performance. When in doubt, consider a quality aftermarket linear PSU — some enthusiasts find it's one of the better upgrades at this tier.
